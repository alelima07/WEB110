Social Media Optimization (SMO)

SMO, short for Social media optimization, is a very important tool to strategically create interesting online content, ranging from well-written text to eye-catching digital photos or video clips that encourages and entices people to engage with a website and then share this content, via its weblink, with their social media contacts and friends.

In other words, SMO is the optimization of brands throughout social media. It makes certain that companies' websites and social media profiles are optimized for social media users through its platform. Have you ever opened a third party link through Facebook and the webpage just did not work or look good through your mobile display? So, that page was not optimized for social media.

Social media has a massive number of users and potential clients. Social media is part of our daily lives, Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, Snapchat, and many more platforms are names that we know and recognize even just by the logo. To show how massive Social media is today, a poll from Pew Research Center shows that 86% of U.S. People aged between 18 and 29 are social media users. Moreover, 69% of them use it at least once a day. So, as web developers, the awareness of optimizing websites for social media is a must.

As web developers, there are some best practices to help improve the social media optimization of websites. The first one is making the loading time of the website faster. Hostway shows steps to improve the loading time of websites. They cover some amazing points like Minify JavaScript and CSS, enable HTTP keep-alive response headers, combining images into CSS sprites and many more. The second one is making sure the website has easy navigation.

Here are some ways to help you improve your SMO.

  • Pick the right strategy.
  • Do keyword research.
  • Optimize social profile. Make it clear and exactly.
  • Content is king. Optimize it.
  • Optimize the content schedule. Need to know the best time to publish your content.
  • Tracking everything. If you don't measure it you can't manage it.
